Shirish's Nov 8 Lunch Menu

A bunch of Nepali or Nepali-inspired dishes and things I know how to make

Jimbu le jhaneko Kodo ko Kholey (Millet Pottage)

With hummus, served with an omlet and raw onion.

Until recently, Kholey meant "warm animal feed" to me. A friend whose family is from Manang, makes rice flour / saatu porridge, and serves it with omlet for breakfast. It really warms me up and I sometimes miss it. I decided to try to replicate that with kodo ko flour.

I noticed adding hummus significantly improves the texture, and makes it tastier. To add a little more complexity, I suggested folks eat it with chopped raw onion.

Mushroom Mapo Tofu

My favourite szechuan-restaurant dish, with mushrooms instead of ground meat to make it vegetarian..

Mapo tofu has been my favourite dish in Chinese restaurants forever. My friends in Virginia have tried making it from scratch, without much success. I used the 'hot' packet from the Asian store, and fried mushrooms for 20 minutes before putting the mix (and tofu) in. I usually add other spices, but was far too busy to do it this time around.

Mixed Chinese Choys

Two types of seasonal greens (Bok choy and Baby Bok Choy), flash-wilted and tempered with mustard seed.

Added a small amount of MSG, and cooked the white parts for quite a while. I've noticed Nepali people tend to enjoy their greens very well-cooked, compared to folks from East Asia.

I've struggled with making good-tasting greens that people will actively appreciate. With this recipe I think I've found my pace!

Mustang Ko Simi ko Dal

A creamy, lentil soup from Mustang beans, with a Mustang Jimbu and dried chillies temper.

These beans were a bit old, so I had to pressure cook them for almost an hour. Because I added so many chillies, the 'body' of the dal was hidden. Mustang ko simi ko dal tastes great, but it's definitely not an everyday kind of dal for me.

Circular slices of Radish, Cucumber & Carrot

Like they do it in Kathmandu.

Because I rarely (read: never) serve actual rice, rice, I wanted folks to get a feeling of comfort. This is the 'salad' they service in all get-together's in Nepal. This was a 'tribute' to that.

Spiced fake Kellewelle

Sweet fried plantains from Trader Joe's, air-fried and mixed with Taijin spice powder.

Because I got robbed -- multiple times -- in Ghana (long story, some other day), I ate what the fellow Ghanian students were eating, because I couldn't afford foreign food. My favourite Ghanian dish was Kellewelle, spicy fried plantain. This was my attempt to replicate that in the most basic way.

Since I discovered Trader Joe's frozen fried plantains, I've (re) introduced it to multiple friends, and they've all appreciated it greatly.

Chinese Titey Karela Sadheko

Nepali-style pickle of Chinese titey karela, sour and spicy, spiced with raw greens (untempered spices).

A recipe that's become one of my classics by this point. I experimented with it about 2 years ago, and was encouraged by how everybody always loved it. I blanced this batch, but also used cheap black tea in the water for the tannins to preserve the crispyness. At the cost of the color (green to brown) it was crispier. An interesting tradeoff to turn toggles on.

Dhaniya & Garlic Chutney

With oven-charred tomatoes, and flax and chia seeds for better texture.

The lauro-silauto-crushed tomato achar in Kathmandu is so great because it's not blitzed by the mixer. I didn't have the energy or equipment to get that texture. So I thought, what if I drop chia seeds and flax seeds, and see if we got something interesting? The texture is definitely thicker, and folks were very enthusiastic about this one! This is such a comfort food for everyone.

Walnut & Roasted Tomato Achaar

A dip to go along with chips, with spring onion and roasted cumin.

This might be the most boojie thing I've ever made. Used coconut water instead of tap water. Just so much good-quality olive oil too. Just the cost of raw ingredients I dumped into this for no good reason boggles the mind. Roasted cumin was a good decision. I might have to revisit the spring onion, it's to fibery.

Air-Fried Red Yeast Tofu

Tofu marinated for 24 hours in red rice yeast, served crisp.

In deep pangs of hunger, I discovered in the recent past that air-fried tofu actually tastes pretty fantastic!
I had red rice yeast to play around with my fermentation projects. In Chinese cuisine, they use it as a rub for meats and vegetable before roasting. I figured it'd make for a worthy experiment, and ran a few experiments a few weeks ago. I quite enjoyed this batch!

Everest Silver Needle Tea

2024 batch, first pick of the season from Everest tea house, supposedly the best tea Nepal has to offer.

In 2024 I went to Everest tea house, and asked what they best teas they had to offer were like. I bought 100g of the Silver Needle Tip Tea, because that was the top-of-the-top, and I felt it'd be worth Rs 1,000 cost just to see what the big deal was.

A friend who never drinks tea apparently said after trying it, that she didn't mind it at all and would like to drink more of it. It was noticeably less dry and astingent than the White tea I'd also brought last year. It was supposed to be decent tea too. We also tried that.

Quinoa ra fapar ko bhat

Quinoa and buckwheat grains cooked together in the rice cooker.

This is what I eat everyday, so not specially exciting for me. Folks were surprised by how I was able to get such intact consistency for quinoa.
