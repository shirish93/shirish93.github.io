Getting ready to graduate

Taking your product to the market

Shirish Pokharel

  • What is ‘graduation’ for a product?
  • When the initial design, prototyping, and testing phase is done
  • Taking it to customers to sell, validate, and get feedback
  • Even if not 100% ready, a demoable prototype is good enough
  • Marketing, and sales takes over from the development team
  • Great time to learn about your assumptions, and market situation!

Why graduate

  • At some point you’ll run out of money developing the product!
  • Or the group funding you will run out of money and/or patience!
  • Because you’re ready to make real money!
  • If your product doesn’t make it to the market, you havelittle to show for your future endeavors
  • You’ll get a good sense of where your future direction should be headed

Preparing mentally for graduation

  • Just like preparing for college graduation, mentally prepare that the world ‘out there’ is going to be harder
  • Understand that there’s going to be a lot of initial hurdles, but you have created a worthy product that the market deserves to get
  • You will need to put on your ‘sales and marketing’ hat on from now on, be prepared for the change in lifestyle
  • There will be a lot of competitors doing similar things, it’s good news! That means the market understands the value of your product
  • Start thinking from the perspective of the future customers, and what they want

What’s next?

  • Prepare a list of all your strengths, and what’s great about your product
  • Think from the customer perspective, not engineering perspective: how will the customer be able to generate $$$ from your features?
  • Prepare a ’competitive advantage’ document: among all of of the competitors in the market, what’s so great about you? What’s your niche?
  • Prepare a technical brochure: a couple of pages (online, physical, any) listing your major features, and how awesome your product is!
  • Prepare a video/real-time demo of your product, using it as your customer would, and explain how the product is generating value

Talking to the King

  • Use your industry leads to identify top 50 potential customers, start with a list of the 10 likeliest ones.
  • Call them and offer to run your demo for them, explaining what your business is
  • Practice the ”sales pitch” AKA what you are going to tell the customer in-person, a hundred times! It better be really good!
  • Meet the customer in person, give them the sales pitch, and follow up after a couple of days. Keep following up until they either buy, or reject you!
  • Even if they don’t want your product, ask if they know anybody who would get value from it, and follow up on them.
